About Me

I am an early-career marketing researcher currently pursuing a fully funded PhD in Business Administration (Marketing) at the KFUPM Business School, King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals, Saudi Arabia. My research lies at the intersection of tourism marketing, place branding, and the role of technology in shaping tourist behavior and destination appeal.

My academic journey spans three countries—Indonesia, Malaysia, and Saudi Arabia—bringing an international perspective to my work. I hold a fully funded MSc in Business Studies (Marketing) from KFUPM and a fully funded Bachelor of Business Administration from Albukhary International University, Malaysia.

My current research examines how AI-driven tools and immersive technologies such as virtual and augmented reality transform tourist experiences, influence visit intentions, and redefine how destinations communicate their identity. I approach these questions with a combination of quantitative methods, quasi-experimental designs, and systematic literature reviews.

Beyond research, I serve as a Junior Lecturer at Darunnajah University, Indonesia, a Co-Managing Editor for the Journal of AI & Immersive Marketing, and founder of Transleto.id—a language-services startup. I am passionate about connecting rigorous academic inquiry with real-world impact in tourism and marketing practice.

Research Interests

My work spans multiple intersecting domains within marketing and tourism scholarship

Tourism Marketing

Investigating how destinations develop competitive marketing strategies and shape tourist experiences.

Place Branding

Examining how places construct and communicate identity to attract visitors and foster loyalty.

Consumer Behavior

Understanding decision-making, motivations, and psychological drivers in tourism and marketing contexts.

Technology Adoption

Studying how tourists and consumers accept and integrate emerging digital technologies into their behavior.

AI in Marketing

Exploring artificial intelligence applications in tourism marketing, heritage storytelling, and personalization.

Immersive Technologies

Researching virtual and augmented reality as tools for transforming destination marketing and tourist engagement.

Virtual Reality in Tourism

Empirically testing how VR experiences influence visit intentions, perceived destination attractiveness, and emotional responses.
